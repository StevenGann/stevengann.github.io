### Life Story

I started programming around 9 years of age. My father was an electrical engineer turned software developer and introduced me to a number of computing technologies including Assembly for PIC16 and PBasic for the Basic Stamp. My parents encouraged me to persue technology and I spent elementary and high school working on simple robots and games using LEGO Mindstorms, QBasic, Blitz3D, and GameMaker. 

I attended Indian River State College for my Associate's in Computer Science, but decided my talents were better suited for hardware than pure software. When I moved on to Florida Atlantic University I switched majors to Computer Engineering and stuck with that through my Master's degree.

After university I spent a couple years working as am embedded developer for Logus Microwave before moving on to Microchip Technology Inc. where I am now.

In my free time I continue working on games and robots, as well as experimenting with AI, blockchain, and the .NET platform.
