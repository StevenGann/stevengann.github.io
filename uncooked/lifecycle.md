# Life Cycle Game concept

### By Steven Gann


## The Premise

An action/survival MMORPG focused on making the most of a life before it ends and restarts.

## Life Cycle

### Childhood

When the cycle starts or restarts the character is "born" into a settlement established and maintained by other players, typically at random. As a child the character is extremely vulnerable and weak, but their choices and actions at this stage will steer their future stats and abilities in profound ways. Children in a settlement will depend on the the adults for protection and exerpeicne buffs, while the children will only be able to effectively contribute to a settlement through labor. As the child levels up, their strength and combat-related stats and abilities grow, allowing them to take care of themselves more effectively. During this phase a child will make many choices of bonuses and abilitiies on skill trees that won't take full effect until adulthood. Making the same choices taken by adults within the settlement are cheaper, allowing more growth if patterned after their caretakers.

Of course, there is no hard rule forcing children to stay with adults. If a child believes they are able to keep themselves fed, warm, and alive in the wild alone they are free to walk away from a settlement and strike out on their own. The complete absence of adults in the nearby area brings its own benefits, lowering the cost of skill tree perks related to wilderness survival and homesteading.

### Adulthood

Once a child has leveled up enough they become an adult and their stat progression slows dramatically, meaning their skill tree and stat choices during childhood will stick with them through the rest of their lives. There is still progression to be made with sufficient grinding, or by engaging in various magicks. How adulthood progresses is largely a matter of player skill and luck, however. Once adulthood is reached, injuries will leave lasting effects and scars unless immediately treated by a skilled healer. A player may live a very long time but find themselves crippled by accumulated injuring and as dependent on the younger adults of a settlement as when they were children. Or, a player may be extremely fortunate and go on indefinitely accumulating more skill and power.

### Death

Death can come at any time regardless of age. If a player is killed and not revived they become a spirit where they died and a reaper will approach them. The player may choose to go peacefully with the reaper and be immediately reborn into a new life with some benefits related to their previous life or some control over where they respawn.

If the player chooses not to go peacefully they can attempt to fight the reaper. If they defeat the reaper their spirit returns to their body and they have another chance at survival though still as wounded or sick as when they died.

As a third alternative, the player may choose to simply flee from the reaper. If they escape and the reaper gives up the chase they become a ghost. A ghost cannot be seen by most players and cannot communicate with most players, with the exception of players with specific magical skills. Ghosts are able to cast buffs and debuffs on the living, and they can see, communicate with, attack, and be attacked by other ghosts. Ghosts can also see and interact with the spirits of recently dead players and the reapers dispatched to collect them. Ghosts may communicate with the spirits, attack them, or help them fight off the reaper.

Ghosts also have a value of territory such that their strength grows the longer they stay in specific area. If their power grows sufficiently they may become visible to living players and able to interact with them so long as they stay within that area.

Ghosts may be destroyed by other ghosts, by players capable of interacting with ghosts, or by reapers. When a ghost is finally destroyed they are reborn into a new life but cursed with debuffs.
