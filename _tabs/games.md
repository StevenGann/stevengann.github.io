## 27 Survivors

![The colony ship Orpheus](/assets/img/games/27-survivors/orpheus-ship.png){: width="1400" height="788" }

> *Wake as one of twenty-seven survivors aboard a generation ship. Investigate the antagonist hunting you. Repair the sabotaged systems keeping you alive. Die — and pass control to the next sleeper. The counter starts at 27. When it reaches zero, the run ends.*

A single-session roguelike mystery-horror set aboard the colony ship *Orpheus*. Twenty-seven survivors ride in shallow hibernation. You wake. The others do not. Something woke you on purpose, and it is still aboard. The look is amber-on-black PSX/N64 — vertex-snapped geometry, affine textures, fog-as-culling, all crushed through a CRT amber HUD.

[![27 Survivors title screen](/assets/img/games/27-survivors/title.jpg){: width="1600" height="670" }](https://store.steampowered.com/app/4773130/27_Survivors/)

### The loop

While the *Orpheus* is alive, three things are happening at once:

- **Investigation.** Five clues — four about identity, one about motive — are scattered across the ship in records, terminals, and audio logs. Three or more unlock the accusation chamber. A correct accusation ends the run on your terms. An incorrect one kills every remaining survivor in a single decision.
- **Repair.** Doors lock. Air recyclers fail. Reactor coolant lines rupture. Each sabotaged system runs on its own clock, patched with parts looted from the bodies you leave behind.
- **The antagonist.** No fixed location, no predictable patrol — a decision engine that scores your behaviour every minute and acts accordingly. Linger in one room and sabotage there grows more likely. Solve a clue and a different kind of pressure arrives.

### One life each

You have one life. When you die, control passes to the next sleeper — a different person, a different set of strengths and weaknesses, the same ship and the same mystery. Each survivor is a real character with a name, an age, and a body that fits their history: a retired structural engineer in her sixties moves and takes punishment differently from a twenty-three-year-old apprentice electrician. The roster is randomized at the start of every run.

Four possible identities for the antagonist. Four possible motives. **Sixteen distinct endings** — the truth is rolled fresh every run, and the clues never spoil the answer; they only constrain it.
