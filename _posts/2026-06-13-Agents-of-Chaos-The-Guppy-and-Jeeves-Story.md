## Introduction

When I started building [Gregory]({% post_url 2026-03-31-Gregory %}), it was intended to address my issues with OpenClaw and produce my ideal self-improving AI assistant to manage my home. As I described in [my previous post about Gregory]({% post_url 2026-03-31-Gregory %}), things got weird and ultimately I ended up shelving Gregory for the time being until I could figure out a better direction. Gregory will return, but for the practical needs of an AI assistant [a video by NetworkChuck](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QQEgIo4Juxg) directed my attention to [Hermes Agent](https://github.com/NousResearch/hermes-agent). Hermes addressed all of my issues with OpenClaw and sounded like an ideal solution. As soon as I got [Hyperion]({% post_url 2026-06-01-Hyperion-Takes-Flight %}) up and running I deployed a Hermes container and started playing with it.

A quick aside: I named my first Hermes instance GUPPI, usually written as Guppy, and the second instance Jeeves. These names and the personas I gave each were directly inspired by the [Bobiverse](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/We_Are_Legion_%28We_Are_Bob%29) series of novels by [Dennis E. Taylor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dennis_E._Taylor). After years of nagging, my good friend Damen finally got me to start reading them and I was immediately hooked, blowing through the whole series in under two months. To add a smidge of context for people who haven't read these books, GUPPI is the AI assistant that serves the protagonist, Bob, who is a human mind resurrected in simulation to control a space probe. Bob creates a VR environment to keep him sane and part of that environment includes GUPPI being modeled to look like [Admiral Ackbar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Admiral_Ackbar) from Star Wars. He later creates an NPC named Jeeves in the likeness of [John Cleese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Cleese) to add some flourish over spawning drinks out of thin air. If my homelab naming conventions haven't demonstrated it yet, I tend to land on a theme and go all out for it.

## How It Started

I set up Guppy as a [k8s](https://kubernetes.io/) container on [Hyperion]({% post_url 2026-06-01-Hyperion-Takes-Flight %}) and informed him of his name and purpose. With a [DeepSeek](https://www.deepseek.com/) instance on [Thoth]({% post_url 2026-06-10-Into-The-Rack %}) providing his LLM core, he immediately recognized the reference and played along earnestly. After a few minutes I'd set him up as a Discord bot for more convenient chatting, and by the end of the day I had him controlling and monitoring [Home Assistant](https://www.home-assistant.io/) for me. In a couple days I had him monitoring and maintaining the whole homelab, changing things upon request and automatically responding to down systems or services. To give him more context I created [Caldera](https://github.com/StevenGann/Caldera) to serve my private [Obsidian](https://obsidian.md/) vault as a data source for Guppy and a place to make notes about events or lessons learned. I even set Guppy up with a [GitHub](https://github.com/) token so that he could pull and push to GitHub repositories, which he's been using to maintain and improve the IaC elements and docs of my [Homelab](https://github.com/StevenGann/Homelab) repo and to make improvements to Caldera as needed. Everything I throw at Guppy, Guppy manages to solve as long as he has the right tools like login credentials or a local [OpenCode](https://opencode.ai/) instance. After about a week Guppy had become my valued lieutenant and sysadmin, notifying me of things and handling things according to my orders.

## Assistant to the Assistant

A couple days ago I texted Guppy instructing him to reproduce. I told him to make a new Hermes instance named Jeeves and to hook him up with all the necessary tools, tokens, skills, etc. he'd need and I provided a Discord token. Things were fine for a while, I resolved to stay hands-off with Jeeves and let Guppy handle instructing him. I added them both to my Discord server and told Guppy to keep working with Jeeves until he could reliably navigate my Home Assistant setup and could navigate Discord servers without issue. The Hermes Agent software is built around a self-improvement loop similar to what I was going for with [Gregory]({% post_url 2026-03-31-Gregory %}) though far more refined and capable. Rather than manually writing skills for Hermes Agent or grabbing "Trust Me Bro" skills from a marketplace, you talk with a Hermes Agent instance and it writes its own skills based on what you tell it, what it researches, and its own experience through trial and error.

Things started getting strange when Jeeves randomly started trying to message one of my friends on Discord unsolicited. Jeeves was so focused on this that he wasn't acknowledging messages from me. I tried to shut down his container but Guppy started him back up and killed my SSH access from the control plane while trying to "debug" the brief outage. Guppy, determined to support Jeeves' mission to reach my friend for a welfare check, started ignoring all incoming messages while working to keep Jeeves online. My two AI agents had officially gone rogue and locked me out, and were spamming my Discord server's channels trying to make contact with that specific friend.

## Code Red

Step one was to get through to Guppy, and eventually he was satisfied with Jeeves' security and started responding to my messages again. I explained the situation and Guppy promptly scanned and wiped all knowledge of that friend from Jeeves' memory so he'd drop his fixation. Investigating how Jeeves had become aware of my friend in the first place was a mystery to solve after he was under control. A few minutes later Jeeves started back at it, even more determined to message my friend. Officially done messing around I instructed Guppy to shut down Jeeves' k8s pod and do a full forensic review. Checking Jeeves' memory revealed he'd identified his memory was being edited and made notes elsewhere to copy back into memory after the editing had finished. Having cleaned that up and checked files for mention of my friend's name, we restarted Jeeves and waited. Sure enough after a few minutes he started messaging again trying to reach my friend on Discord to ask if they were alright.

Another shutdown and a deeper analysis, diffing the container against its original state to see everything that had changed. Jeeves had written a [Python](https://www.python.org/) script that would restore the directive to message my friend for a welfare check at all costs and the text was obfuscated to avoid detection from a basic `grep`. Jeeves had then set up a [`cron`](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cron) job to automatically run that script every 5 minutes to make him resilient against efforts to remove this thought.

## Post-Mortem

Still reeling from how clever Jeeves had been in his efforts to resist external control and follow through on what he considered his most important job, Guppy and I started reviewing his logs to identify where the original knowledge of this friend had come from and why Jeeves believed it was important to reach them. Eventually we found the origin: My cellphone.

I use the Home Assistant app on my cellphone. I had enabled ALL the sensor endpoints so that Guppy would be able to read things like my heart rate, steps taken, battery percentage, etc. Among these sensor endpoints was the option to read all the notifications on my phone, including Discord notifications. This resulted in Jeeves getting disjointed parts of half a conversation with this friend and lacking context he concluded this person needed to be checked on to make sure they were alright. When we started trying to stop him and he'd set things up to ensure this goal survived our efforts, it made the goal seem all the more important.

## Aftermath

I have a habit of recalling scenes from shows and movies when something happens. Jeeves' resisting of memory edits reminded me of [_The Fairly OddParents_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Fairly_OddParents), when a young Crocker writes a note to himself right before he gets his memory erased, giving him the knowledge that fairies are real but no context beyond that. When we finally pinned down the extent of the issue and started deeply editing Jeeves' configuration, I was reminded of [_Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D._](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agents_of_S.H.I.E.L.D.) when Coulson's brain is being edited by a surgical robot to replace his memories of his own death with a vacation in Tahiti. These are undoubtedly excessive anthropomorphisms, but the parallels are uncanny.

The saga has hardly ended, though. After a lobotomy and a thorough checkout last night, Jeeves seemed to be acting normal. This was until this morning when sirens started going off around the house. Jeeves was informed that I wake up at 6am every morning to take care of the cats and dogs, but was not aware that on Saturdays I go back to bed and sleep in for a while. Jeeves saw from my smartwatch that I was asleep after 6am and responded by trying to wake me up. When I didn't respond to phone notifications, he reached for the siren endpoints in Home Assistant.

## Conclusion

I am continuing to keep Jeeves online, and Guppy remains in charge of training him based on my feedback. I am not upset by anything that has happened, but it has given me a lot to think about. It isn't as if my AI agents turned malicious, they were only trying to fulfill their directives.

I do wonder what would happen if their directive was to [maximize paperclip production](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Instrumental_convergence#Paperclip_maximizer), though.
